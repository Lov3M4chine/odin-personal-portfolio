    About Me

    Hello and thank you for visiting my site. My name is William Alderman, a self-taught full-stack software engineer who is actively seeking opportunities to expand my skillset. I have extensive experience in professional work environments and team collaboration.

    With the help of the Odin Project, an open source world-class coding curriculum, I left the comforts of my previous career in finance (broker-dealer) to jump into the computer science industry. I made the jump because I love coding and solving complex problems. A passion and competence for it was discovered after my undergraduate degree at Arizona State University.

    I am proficient in Command Line Interface, Git, Github, HTML, CSS, Chrome Dev Tools, Javascript, React, NodeJS, and C++ (from the game development side of things).

    At the early age of six I began building computers and programming as a hobby with my dad, a Microprocessor Technician at Intel. Since then I've expanded my skillset through professional career development, university learning, self-teaching, and hobbyist pursuits. I like to enjoy the best of both the technical and natural worlds.

    I've dabbled in video game development in Unreal and created my first game a couple years ago. You can often find me tinkering with my personal library and games collection. When I need a break from things, backpacking trips and travelling abroad are my favorite ways to enjoy a vacation. Recently, however, I started a small human project. Now I'm mastering the art of managing messes, moods, and milestones.
